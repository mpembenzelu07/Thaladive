Safety Rules

  • Never dive alone - always maintain buddy contact
  • Monitor your air supply - begin ascent with 50 bar remaining
  • Perform safety stop at 5 meters for 3 minutes
  • Maintain neutral buoyancy at all times
  • Equalize early and often during descent

Emergency Procedures

Out of Air: Signal to your buddy, ascend together using buddy's alternate air source
Lost Buddy: Search for 1 minute, then surface and reunite on boat
Decompression Sickness: Report any symptoms immediately to dive master

Equipment Check

  • BCD inflator/deflator functions properly
  • Regulator delivers air smoothly
  • Alternate air source accessible
  • Weight system releases easily
  • Mask strap adjusted and sealed
  • Fins fit properly and comfortably

Equipment Setup

Our dive masters will help you set up your equipment. If you're using our rental gear, it will be prepared for you with:

  • Tank filled to 200 bar
  • Regulator assembled and tested
  • BCD adjusted to your size
  • Weight calculated for your buoyancy

Mbudya Island Site

Mbudya Island features beautiful coral gardens starting at 3 meters and descending to 15 meters. The site is protected from strong currents, making it ideal for beginner and intermediate divers.

Site Details

  • Depth: 3-15m
  • Visibility: 20-30m
  • Temp: 26-29Â°C
  • Dive Time: 45 min

Conditions

  • Current: Light to none
  • Light: Excellent
  • Entry: Boat, back roll
  • Bottom: Sandy with coral

Dive Procedures

Descent Procedure

  1. Giant stride entry from boat
  2. Signal OK to dive master
  3. Equalize every meter
  4. Follow anchor line to bottom
  5. Regroup at 5 meters

Ascent Procedure

  1. Begin ascent with 50 bar remaining
  2. Ascend slowly (9m per minute)
  3. Safety stop at 5m for 3 minutes
  4. Surface near boat ladder
  5. Signal OK upon surfacing

Marine Life

Mbudya Island is home to diverse marine life including colorful reef fish, invertebrates, and occasional larger species.

Common Sightings

  • Butterflyfish
  • Angelfish
  • Clownfish
  • Parrotfish
  • Starfish
  • Moray eels

Seasonal Sightings

  • Green turtles
  • Eagle rays
  • Reef sharks
  • Octopus
  • Lobsters
  • Barracuda schools
Do Not Touch: Look but don't touch marine life. Coral is fragile and some creatures can be dangerous.
